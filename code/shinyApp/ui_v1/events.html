Relevant Current Events

The US is expanding CO2 pipelines. One poisoned town wants you to know its story

The article highlights concerns from a town in Illinois where residents are facing risks from a CO2 pipeline project. A controversial part of the infrastructure involves the potential for leaks and the hazards associated with transporting CO2 through populated areas. As residents voice their concerns, they contend with the industry's promises of carbon capture as a climate solution, which some experts argue may be more about preserving fossil fuel interests than addressing the climate crisis effectively. The article emphasizes the dangers of not fully addressing the health and safety impacts on local communities.

ADM pauses CO2 injection at carbon capture storage site after finding potential leak

The Reuters article reports that ADM has paused CO2 injection at a carbon capture and storage site in Illinois after detecting potential issues that might affect the safety of the operation. The pause follows concerns about the site's integrity and risks related to the injection process. ADM is working with regulators to investigate and resolve the issues before resuming operations.

Costly climate ‘solutions’ look like more pollution in Louisiana’s ‘Cancer Alley’

The article discusses residents of St. Rose, Louisiana, fighting against the construction of a "blue" ammonia plant linked to carbon capture technology, which is marketed as environmentally friendly but criticized for its reliance on fossil fuels. Local organizers and scientists argue that the project could worsen pollution and health risks in an already struggling community, while tax credits and federal support back the initiative despite skepticism about the technology's effectiveness in combating climate change.

Biden-Harris Administration Invests $251 Million to Expand Infrastructure to Support CO2 Transport and Storage

The Biden-Harris administration has announced an investment of $251 million to enhance infrastructure for carbon dioxide (CO2) transport and storage. This funding supports 12 projects across seven states, aiming to advance carbon management and help reduce CO2 emissions.
